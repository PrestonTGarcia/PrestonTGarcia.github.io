## A Strong Leader?

One day before all this coronavirus mess I was playing a game with a friend, and we were ironically talking about how we could improve our work ethic and pave our way to success. Somewhere in the conversation, my friend told me "You know, you would make a strong leader."

Although I stayed quiet as a response, part of me was in shock. At the time, I was lazy, all I did was play games and scraped by when it came to grades. I failed as a leader many times in the past, such as when I was the president of my High School's Chess Club. At the time, I was scared of leadership because of my failures as a leader. Despite this, I set out to research and find out why I was seen as a good leader, and what makes a good leader.

After taking a whole course studying leadership styles and developing my own philosophy of leadership, I could finally answer why I was seen as a good leader. I believe that I was good at serving others.

## Servant? Leader?

Leaders are often to be seen as a dominant force or person in a society, organization or group; however, I believe that the best way to lead is to serve those that you lead. Although "servant" and "leader" seem to have opposite meanings, I believe a strong way to be a leader is to be a servant. I always look back at leaders who are successful, and one thing I've always found in common is that these leaders took initiative and made an impact to serve those that they led, and that brought them to success.

Take for example, Bill Gates, co-founder of Microsoft, successful entrepreneur and business leader. Bill Gates could be seen as a servant leader, as most of his endeavors relate to serving others. Bill Gates' initial vision when creating Microsoft was to provide ease of access to communication for individuals and businesses. In this way, Gates is attempting to serve individuals and businesses of society by developing Windows. Another case where Gates is seen as a servant leader is his creation of the Bill and Melinda foundation. This foundation was made to help those in need, attempting to lead others to healthier lives.

Servant leadership is a strong, and productive way to lead others, as the main goal is to serve others and help them grow and become better people.

## So what?

I could talk about leadership and how I could be a strong leader a good amount, but that begs the question, so what? How can I apply all this to the real world, and in my pursuit to become a computational physicist?

Just like Bill Gates, I plan to work towards a vision and a mission to serve others. I plan to be a servant leader when it comes to group projects in my college classes, or even when I lead other computational physicists and software developers in the future. I can do this by ensuring that I'm fulfilling those that I lead's wants and needs, through different processes like voting or taking input on what we think is important and what isn't.

Wherever I end up with my career, whether that be as a software engineer or a computational physicist, I plan to keep my servant leadership. I intend on serving myself, those I work with, and the world we live in.
